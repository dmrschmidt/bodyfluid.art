⎯ Statement of practice

One room,
five papers,
the body.

The studio is a small north-facing room on a quiet street in Neukölln, Berlin. Each plate begins with a single sheet of saturated coloured paper laid flat on the floor — and the body, present, unrehearsed. The trace is offered, not performed.

What the camera records is the brief life of a form on a coloured ground — a few seconds, sometimes less, before the paper begins to drink it back. The work is photographed straight, in available daylight.

Plates are produced as pigment giclée prints on Hahnemühle Photo Rag Baryta, 315 gsm. Hand-numbered, signed in graphite, shipped flat with a letterpress certificate of authenticity. Editions are typically seven plus two artist proofs.
